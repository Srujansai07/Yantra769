### 1. DIGITAL LOGIC & COMPUTATIONAL ARCHITECTURE

#### 1.1 Pingala's Binary System (Chandas Shastra, ~300 BCE)

**Primary Source Text:** Chandaḥśāstra by Pingala Acharya

**Core Concepts for Integration:**

**A. Laghu-Guru Binary Framework**
- Laghu (लघु) = Short syllable = Binary 0
- Guru (गुरु) = Long syllable = Binary 1
- **Application:** Fundamental flip-flop circuit design, memory cell architecture

**B. Meru Prastara (Pascal's Triangle)**
- Combinatorial algorithms predating Western discovery by 1500+ years
- **Applications:**
  - Cryptographic algorithms for secure chip communication
  - Data compression algorithms in memory controllers
  - Error correction codes (ECC) in RAM
  - Binomial coefficient calculations in AI inference engines

**C. Pratyaya Algorithm (Prastaar)**
- Systematic enumeration of all binary combinations
- **Applications:**
  - Test vector generation for chip validation
  - State machine enumeration in digital design
  - Exhaustive testing algorithms for IC verification

**D. Modern Integration Points:**
- Binary decision diagrams (BDDs) in logic synthesis
- Quine-McCluskey algorithm optimization using Vedic combinatorics
- Look-up table (LUT) architectures in FPGAs

### 2. ATOMIC PHYSICS & MATERIAL SCIENCE

#### 2.1 Vaisheshika Atomism (Kanada, ~600 BCE)

**Primary Source Text:** Vaiśeṣika Sūtra by Kanada (Kaṇāda)
**Commentary:** Praśastapāda's Padārthadharmasaṅgraha

**Core Atomic Principles:**

**A. Paramāṇu (परमाणु) - The Indivisible Atom**
- Sutras 7.1.20: "The eternal is parimandala (spherical)"
- Atomic theory 2000+ years before Dalton
- **Properties Defined:**
  - Spherical geometry (Parimandala)
  - Application: Quantum dot geometry in semiconductor nanofabrication
  - States of motion (Nitya sthiti → Kriyā)
  - Application: Carrier mobility in semiconductors

**B. Dvyanuka & Tryanuka (Diatomic/Triatomic combinations)**
- Molecular bond formation
- **Applications:**
  - Silicon lattice crystal structure
  - Dopant atom placement (phosphorus, boron in silicon)
  - Compound semiconductor engineering (GaN, SiC)

**C. Adrishta (अदृष्ट) - Unseen Forces**
- Gravitational and electromagnetic forces
- **Applications:**
  - Van der Waals forces in 2D materials (graphene, MoS₂)
  - Quantum tunneling effects in nanoscale transistors
  - Electrostatic discharge (ESD) protection circuits

**D. Paramāṇu Size Calculations**
- Ancient texts suggest: 1 Aṅgula = 8⁵ = 262,144 paramanus
- 1 paramanu ≈ 7.2 × 10⁻⁹ meters (within quantum scale!)
- **Relevance:** Approaching atomic layer deposition (ALD) scales

- Baudhayana 1.9: "The diagonal of a square produces double the area"

- Stated 300 years before Pythagoras (570 BCE)

**B. Pythagorean Triples for VLSI:**
- (3, 4, 5), (5, 12, 13), (8, 15, 17), (12, 35, 37)
- **Applications:**
  - Orthogonal routing in IC layout
  - Manhattan distance calculations in place-and-route
  - Signal integrity-aware floorplanning

**C. Altar Geometry Shapes:**
- **Syena Citi (Falcon-shaped):** Multi-layer IC interconnect design
- **Kurma Citi (Tortoise):** ESD protection ring structures
- **Rathachakra Citi (Chariot wheel):** Clock distribution networks

**D. Square Root Approximations:**
- √2 ≈ 1 + 1/3 + 1/(3×4) - 1/(3×4×34) = Accurate to 5 decimal places!
- **Application:** Fast inverse square root in graphics processors
